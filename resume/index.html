About Me

I received the B.S. degree from the Shandong University of Science and Technology, in 2016, and the M.S. degree from Shandong University of Science and Technology, in 2020. I am currently a researcher and NLP algorithm engineer with the AI Research Institute, Shumei Technology, China. My main research interests include Natural Language Processing, Deep Learning and Knowledge Graph. Also, I do some research about Process Mining, such as next event prediction. I focus on using AI to solve practical problems. I am actively seeking the opportunity of PhD(I'm more in the NLP direction but I'm also open to all other opportunities) and I can fund myself.

Student(Master’s Degree) & Researcher, SDUST Distributed Intelligent Information Service Lab
Graduation project for the master’s degree: Business Process Instance Remaining Time Prediction Using Deep Learning, 09/2017-06/2020
Position: Shandong Province, China
Supervisor: Associate Prof. Weijian Ni

  • Enhanced the raw log through multiple traditional process models simulation to address the limited-volume and sparsity issue of event logs; developed a vector representation learning algorithm capable of learning both activity vectors and temporal interval vectors from the enhanced event log based on differences between events in business process and instances of the word in sentences.
  • Modeled the context information of business process instances by bi-directional recurrent neural networks; assigned different weights to each step using attention mechanism; designed a transfer learning framework for remaining time prediction to construct multiple models that trace event log with different lengths.
  • Evaluated the effectiveness of the temporal activity vector and the transfer learning framework for remaining time prediction; improved the prediction accuracy holistically.
  • We published three papers in the filed of Business Process Management, two of which were published on Chinese Conference on Business Process Management(CBPM, the top conference on BPM in China) and these two papers were recommended to the journal Computer Integrated Manufacturing Systems.
